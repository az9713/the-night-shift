# SPIN CYCLE — a 30-second trailer for a Quentin Tarantino film

Complete, render-ready shot plan for an AI text-to-video generator (Seedance / Veo / Kling class: native audio, ~5–10 s clips, stitched).

## Research note — practices applied

Current text-to-video guidance (Kling/Runway/Seedance prompt guides, 2026): keep every clip ≤8 s and stitch; write structured prompts (these shots use tight 90–150-word blocks; guides tolerate up to ~300) naming subject, environment, lighting, and an explicit camera move with speed/direction (e.g. "slow dolly-in," "orbit at 30°/s") rather than vague motion; for native audio, keep speaker name, verbatim line, and delivery description together in one block; generators have no memory between clips, so character wardrobe, location, and grade descriptors are restated verbatim in every shot prompt — never "same as before."

## The scene

**Title: SPIN CYCLE**

```
INT. WASH-O-RAMA COIN LAUNDRY — 2 A.M.

A dead-empty 1970s laundromat. Mint-green walls, checkerboard
floor, one row of avocado-green washers. One machine churns.

FOLEY, 50s, silver-flecked hair slicked back, white undershirt,
suspenders hanging, watches the porthole like it's television.
DUKE, 30s, mustard bowling shirt, gold chain, can't sit still.

                    DUKE
          Cold water. You always do cold water.

                    FOLEY
          Hot sets it. Any child knows hot sets it.

                    DUKE
          It's a lot of... marinara.

                    FOLEY
          Then we run it twice.

LORRAINE, 60s, floral housecoat, cigarette behind her ear, mops
past without looking up.

                    LORRAINE
          Machine eight's better for marinara.
          Takes quarters.

The men turn their heads to her in slow unison. The machine
BUZZES — final spin. Red-tinged water drains behind the glass.

Foley rises. Opens the door. Holds a spotless white dress shirt
up to the fluorescent light. Admires it.

                    FOLEY
          Good as new. Shame about the tie.

SMASH TO BLACK. TITLE: SPIN CYCLE.
```

Three-act micro-arc: setup (two men, one washing machine, laundry small-talk), turn (Lorraine knows exactly what "marinara" is; the machine finishes), button ("Shame about the tie.").

## Shot list

### Shot 1 — Duration: 5s

**Video prompt:** Exterior night, 2 a.m. A buzzing neon sign reading "WASH-O-RAMA — 24 HR" glows pink and green above a small 1970s laundromat storefront, its light reflected in wet black asphalt. Slow dolly-in toward the glass door, steady speed, low camera height. Through the window, fluorescent-lit interior with a row of avocado-green washing machines. Saturated 1990s film look, 35 mm grain, deep shadows, wet-street reflections. Moody, patient, ominous tone. No people visible.

**Dialogue / audio:** No dialogue. Audio: low surf-rock guitar tremolo builds under city-night ambience (distant traffic, neon buzz).

**Continuity note:** Establishes location and grade every later shot must match: mint-green laundromat, avocado washers, checkerboard floor, flickering fluorescents, saturated 35 mm night grade.

### Shot 2 — Duration: 6s

**Video prompt:** Interior, 1970s laundromat at night: mint-green walls, checkerboard floor, row of avocado-green washers under flickering fluorescent tubes. Wide symmetrical shot from floor level, low angle, camera static. Two men sit side by side on plastic chairs facing one churning washing machine. Left: FOLEY, 50s, silver-flecked slicked-back hair, white undershirt, black slacks, suspenders hanging at his hips. Right: DUKE, 30s, mustard-yellow bowling shirt, thin gold chain, leg bouncing. Both stare at the machine like it's television. Saturated color, 35 mm grain, green-tinted fluorescent light.

**Dialogue / audio:** DUKE (edgy, quiet): "Cold water. You always do cold water." FOLEY (flat, certain, not looking at him): "Hot sets it. Any child knows hot sets it." Under: washing-machine churn, fluorescent hum.

**Continuity note:** Character looks defined here are canonical — repeat wardrobe, hair, and seating (Foley left, Duke right) exactly in shots 3 and 6.

### Shot 3 — Duration: 5s

**Video prompt:** 1970s laundromat interior at night: mint-green walls, checkerboard floor, row of avocado-green washers under flickering fluorescent tubes. Tight two-shot, slow dolly-in at constant speed, eye level, on two seated men. Left: FOLEY, 50s, silver-flecked slicked-back hair, white undershirt, black slacks, suspenders hanging at his hips. Right: DUKE, 30s, mustard-yellow bowling shirt, thin gold chain. Duke glances sideways at the churning avocado-green washing machine and swallows; Foley never moves his eyes off the porthole. Green fluorescent key light from above, warm practical glow from the machine's porthole flickering red-tinted on their faces. Saturated 35 mm look, shallow depth of field, tense close-up energy.

**Dialogue / audio:** DUKE (lowering his voice, uneasy): "It's a lot of... marinara." FOLEY (calm, final): "Then we run it twice." Machine churn continues; a single low bass note under the line.

**Continuity note:** Match shot 2 wardrobe, hair, seating sides, green fluorescent grade; the red porthole flicker introduced here recurs in shot 5.

### Shot 4 — Duration: 5s

**Video prompt:** 1970s laundromat interior at night: mint-green walls, checkerboard floor, row of avocado-green washers under flickering green fluorescent tubes. Lateral tracking shot, steady walking pace, camera at waist height moving right-to-left with LORRAINE, 60s, gray hair pinned up, floral housecoat, unlit cigarette behind her ear, pushing a mop across the checkerboard floor in the foreground. Behind her, in focus mid-ground, two men on plastic chairs — on the left chair FOLEY, 50s, silver-flecked slicked-back hair, white undershirt, black slacks, suspenders hanging at his hips; on the right chair DUKE, 30s, mustard-yellow bowling shirt, thin gold chain — turn their heads toward her in slow perfect unison as she passes. She never looks up from the floor. Saturated 35 mm grade, deadpan composition.

**Dialogue / audio:** LORRAINE (bored, not looking up, mid-mop): "Machine eight's better for marinara. Takes quarters." Mop-water slosh, machine churn; the surf guitar stops dead after her line — beat of silence.

**Continuity note:** Men remain seated in shot-2 positions and wardrobe; Lorraine's housecoat and cigarette must match shot 6 background if she appears; keep checkerboard floor and green fluorescent grade.

### Shot 5 — Duration: 5s

**Video prompt:** Extreme close-up on the round glass porthole of an avocado-green 1970s washing machine, camera static, slight low angle. Inside, laundry tumbles through its final spin as red-tinged water sheets down the glass and drains away. The drum slows, thumps, stops. A red indicator lamp above the door clicks off; the buzzer sounds. Reflection of flickering green fluorescent tubes curves across the glass. Saturated 35 mm grain, macro detail on water droplets, ominous stillness after the drum halts.

**Dialogue / audio:** No dialogue. Audio: drum spin-down, heavy mechanical CLUNK, harsh electric BUZZER, then near-silence — one water drip, fluorescent hum.

**Continuity note:** Machine is the same avocado-green unit the men face in shots 2–3; red-tinged water pays off shot 3's red porthole flicker; silence carries into shot 6.

### Shot 6 — Duration: 5s

**Video prompt:** 1970s laundromat interior at night: mint-green walls, checkerboard floor, row of avocado-green washers under flickering green fluorescent tubes. Low-angle medium shot, camera static, looking slightly up: FOLEY, 50s, silver-flecked slicked-back hair, white undershirt, black slacks, suspenders hanging at his hips, stands before the open avocado-green washer and holds a spotless white dress shirt up high against the flickering fluorescent light, inspecting it like a jeweler with a diamond, faint satisfied nod. Behind him on the right-side plastic chair sits DUKE, 30s, mustard-yellow bowling shirt, thin gold chain, staring. Steam rises faintly from the open drum. Saturated 35 mm grade, hero-shot framing. At the end of the shot, hard cut to a black frame with bold yellow condensed capital letters: "SPIN CYCLE".

**Dialogue / audio:** FOLEY (dry, admiring the shirt, almost tender): "Good as new. Shame about the tie." One-beat pause — then a hard surf-rock sting slams in with the title card.

**Continuity note:** Foley's wardrobe/hair match shots 2–3; Duke seated in shot-2 position; open machine is the shot-5 unit; title card style: black background, yellow condensed caps (90s crime-film poster look).
