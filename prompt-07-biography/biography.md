# Pat Simmons — a biography, opening paragraph

In 2022, Pat Simmons was a freelance marketer whose work had run through PepsiCo, Ogilvy, and GSK — a man who sold attention, not algorithms — when he encountered GPT-3. "I realized something was about to fundamentally change," he would later write. What distinguished Simmons was not that he saw the change coming; half the internet claimed that. It was that he refused to pretend to be something he wasn't. He built no AI tools — by his own insistence, his work stayed "strictly advisory and strategy" — and he turned that outsider's honesty into a method. On his YouTube channel, under the banner he called AI for Mortals, he promised knowledge workers "non-technical AI tools and workflows" that could make them "the AI go-to person" on their teams. Then he went further: if the labs' leaderboards couldn't be trusted, he would build his own. SimmonsBench set frontier models loose on more than sixty identical real-world tasks — games, websites, knowledge work, writing — and published what each one actually built, alongside what it cost, under a credo that doubled as a manifesto: "Don't trust the score. See what each model actually built." In an industry drunk on benchmarks, Simmons wagered that ordinary people deserved to see the work.
